Interactive Guide to IELTS Process Essays

Master the structure for Task 1 with this step-by-step guide.

The Introduction

Your first paragraph has one simple job: to introduce the process diagram to the reader. You should paraphrase the information given in the prompt, using your own words to state what the diagram shows. Aim for one or two clear sentences.

Purpose:

To introduce the diagram and the process it illustrates.

Key Action:

Paraphrase the topic sentence from the question.

Examples:

"The diagram illustrates the various stages involved in the production of X."

"The provided flowchart clearly depicts how X is manufactured through a series of steps."

The Overview

The overview provides the "big picture" of the process without going into detail. It should be a short paragraph of about two sentences that highlights the most important features, such as the total number of steps and whether the process is linear (has a start and an end) or cyclical (repeats in a loop).

Purpose:

To present the most prominent features without specific details.

Key Information:

  • State the total number of steps or stages.
  • Identify if the process is linear or cyclical.
  • (Optional) Mention main groupings of activities.

Examples:

Linear: "Overall, the process comprises eight stages, beginning with the collection of raw materials and concluding with the final packaged product."

Cyclical: "In general, the five-step cycle starts when glass bottles are collected and finishes when new products are ready for sale, restarting the process."

The Body Paragraphs

This is where you describe each step of the process in detail. Logically divide the process into two parts and dedicate one paragraph to each part. Use sequencing words to guide the reader through the stages clearly and use the passive voice to keep the focus on the process itself.

Body Paragraph 1

Describe the first half of the process, from the starting point to a logical midway point.

Key Focus:

Detail the raw materials and the first 2-4 steps.

Example Language:

"The process begins when... / First of all, the ingredients are mixed... Following this, the mixture is..."

Body Paragraph 2

Describe the second half of the process, continuing from where you left off and proceeding to the final outcome.

Key Focus:

Detail the final processing and the end product or the start of the loop.

Example Language:

"Subsequently, the product is packaged... / Once this stage has been completed... Finally, the cycle repeats itself..."

Essential Language Checklist

Using the right vocabulary and grammar is crucial for a high score. The passive voice is often used to emphasize the action, and sequencing words are essential for clarity. Click each category below to see examples.

Function: To link steps in a clear, logical order.

First, Next, Then, Following this, Subsequently, After which, Finally, The next phase

Function: To show when an action starts or finishes relative to another.

Once the raw materials are collected, Before the product is packaged, When the substance is heated

Function: To emphasize the action/product (Passive is critical and most common).

The material is transported to... / It is heated to... / The factory packages the final product.

Function: To use specific verbs to describe actions accurately.

Filter, Sort, Transport, Collect, Process, Manufacture, Package, Harvest, Grind, Refine
